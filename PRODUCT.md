# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are people in high-leverage speaking situations who practice with intent:
- Job seekers running drills the night before an interview — anxious, focused, short on time.
- Founders and SDRs sharpening a pitch before a consequential call — results-oriented, habitual.
- Students in debate, communications, or business programs — accountable to improvement over a semester.
- Anyone working on delivery (pacing, filler reduction, on-camera presence) who wants a data-backed loop rather than vague encouragement.

What they have in common: they are not casually exploring. They want to finish a session knowing specifically what to work on before the next one.

## Product Purpose

Clarity is a real-time communication coach that records how you sound and look while you practice, analyzes your delivery through video, audio, and language simultaneously, and turns that signal into scores, replays, and concrete next steps — before your next interview, pitch, or presentation.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a transcript analyzer. It is a structured rehearsal loop.

Three modes:
- **Emotion Sprint**: short scenario-based drill targeting a specific emotional register (confidence, warmth, authority). Scores delivery against target affect.
- **Conversation**: multi-turn session simulating an interview, negotiation, or Q&A. AI plays the other side. Audio and video analyzed in aligned windows.
- **Free Speaking**: uninterrupted recording with a scorecard on pacing, fillers, repetition, emotional arc, and plain-English next steps.

## Brand Personality

Name: Clarity
Tagline: "Practice out loud. See yourself clearly." (alts: "The rehearsal loop you've been missing." / "Real feedback. Before it counts.")

Personality: Calm, grounded, encouraging without being soft. Gives you the truth about your delivery in a way that feels supportive rather than clinical. A trusted coach who respects your time — direct, warm, evidence-based. Does not celebrate mediocrity.

Visual direction: Soft, warm, minimal. Cream and warm white backgrounds. Nothing sharp, cold, or corporate. Closer to Headspace or a premium wellness app than a SaaS dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Corporate HR tools (cold, institutional)
- Clinical medical interfaces (sterile, form-heavy)
- Purple-gradient AI startups (generic, over-branded)
- Gamified apps with streaks and emoji rewards (Duolingo energy, kids-app feel)
- Anything that reads "AI product" at a glance — no neon accents, no glassmorphism hero sections

## Design Principles

1. **Truth, delivered kindly.** Feedback surfaces should feel like a good coach, not a report card. Honest signal without clinical distance.
2. **Signal over chrome.** Every visual element earns its place against whether it helps the user understand their performance or navigate to practice. Decoration is subtracted, not added.
3. **Calm focus.** The interface should lower anxiety, not raise it. A person running drills before a stressful interview should feel settled, not stimulated.
4. **Progress is visible without being gamified.** Trend data and session history matter — but no streaks, no badges, no confetti. Growth is shown, not celebrated.
5. **Practice mode feels different from review mode.** Recording flows are immersive and distraction-free. Scorecard/replay views are clear and scannable. These are distinct contexts, not the same shell with different content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion support (Framer Motion `useReducedMotion`). Sufficient contrast on cream backgrounds — do not assume white-on-cream is always safe. No color-only encoding for feedback scores.
